Platform Investment Thesis
The U.S. medical spa market represents a $44.83B fragmented opportunity with sub-5% PE penetration, 40–60% cash-pay EBITDA margins, and no dominant national brand. Structural analog to Dental DSO and Dermatology pre-consolidation.
~80M Americans are addressable consumers for aesthetics procedures; only 15M have had one.1 Gen Z and Millennials — 36% of adult U.S. population — drive 41% of annual wellness spend and are aging into the core demographic.2

Figure 4.C — Value Creation Playbook
Three levers drive EBITDA expansion post-acquisition
01 — Digital Infrastructure Standardization
Shared booking platform, CRM, and loyalty/membership program deployed across all locations. Online booking (col BO) and membership program (col BN) are already present in top-scoring targets — scaling these reduces customer acquisition cost and builds recurring revenue base.
02 — Service Menu Expansion
Single-service operators (injectables only) expanded to full menu (laser, body contouring, skin resurfacing). Service count (col V) is a direct proxy for revenue potential — operators with V >3 are estimated to generate 40–60% more revenue per location than single-service peers.
03 — Brand Premium and Medical Director Leverage
Medical director presence (col BS/BT) on platform enables proper clinical oversight across locations. Platform brand commands premium pricing vs. independent competitors. Medical Director on-file (col X) is a quality signal present in 68% of top STOC-scoring targets.
