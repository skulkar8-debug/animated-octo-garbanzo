Target Zone Framework (Z1 – Z4)

Each location is classified into a Target Zone based on proximity to existing supply, adjusted for local market density, to inform whether it is best suited for bolt‑on, tuck‑in, or de novo deployment.[file:31]

Z1 – Highly Saturated / Immediate Competition
Very close to existing supply in dense markets; highest overlap and cannibalization risk.
Ideal for competitive takeouts and selective bolt‑ons where brand and Digital Maturity are strong.[file:31]
Z2 – Tuck‑in / Cluster Opportunity
Close to existing supply but still viable for add‑ons, balancing demand and competition.[file:31]
Supports regional clustering strategies and operational leverage.
Z3 – Transitional / Emerging
Emerging markets with moderate supply and growing demand (not prominent in this sample but supported by the logic framework).
Z4 – De Novo / White Space
High Supply Gap and limited nearby providers; ideal for greenfield sites and anchor platforms in mid‑density or suburban CBSAs.[file:31]

PE Playbook: How to Use This Map

  • Platform Selection: Prioritize CBSAs with multiple Z2/Z4 locations, STOCC Scores in the top decile, and a mix of Prime Opportunity and digitally mature assets.[file:31]
  • Bolt‑ons & Tuck‑ins: Target Z1/Z2 sites with high Digital Maturity (>70) to accelerate scale and leverage existing brand equity.[file:31]
  • De Novo Rollout: Use high STOCC + positive Supply Gap + Z4 to rank order new build markets and sequence capital deployment.[file:31]
  • Exit Narrative: Build local density in high‑score CBSAs, then arbitrage from small‑asset multiples into platform multiples through strategic sale or secondary PE.[file:31]
